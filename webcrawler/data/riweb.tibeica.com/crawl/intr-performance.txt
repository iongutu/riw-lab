  <h1><a name="SECTION003100000000000000000">&nbsp;</a> <br> 1.1 Performance </h1> 
  <p> One of the main advantages of mod_python is the increase in performance over traditional CGI. Below are results of a very crude test. The test was done on a 1.2GHz Pentium machine running Red Hat Linux 7.3. <em class="citetitle"><a href="http://httpd.apache.org/docs-2.0/programs/ab.html" title="Ab">Ab</a></em> was used to poll 4 kinds of scripts, all of which imported the standard cgi module (because this is how a typical Python cgi script begins), then output a single word "<tt class="samp">Hello!</tt>". The results are based on 10000 requests with concurrency of 1. </p>
  <p> </p>
  <div class="verbatim">
   <pre>
    Standard CGI:               23 requests/s
    Mod_python cgihandler:     385 requests/s
    Mod_python publisher:      476 requests/s
    Mod_python handler:       1203 requests/s
</pre>
  </div>
